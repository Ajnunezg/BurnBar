# Insights 2.0 — Master Plan

> **The promise:** Open the Insights tab on any of iPhone, iPad, Mac, or Android and within 500ms see a single sentence that proves the app knows you, a ring that tells you whether today is on track, and a session trace that reads like an X-ray of your week. Then — only then — explore the canvas.

**Date:** 2026-05-16
**Version:** 2.0 (successor to `plans/2026-05-12-insights-tab-1.0.md`)
**Status:** Plan — ready for an implementation swarm
**Platforms:** iOS · iPadOS · macOS (AgentLens) · Android · watchOS · Wear OS · widgets · notifications · share-sheet · PDF · MP4
**Predecessor:** v1.0 shipped the **scaffolding**. v2.0 ships the **product**.
**Author:** Claude (Opus 4.7)

## 0. TL;DR

Insights v1.0 is **80% complete infrastructurally** and **40% hollow experientially**. The types, executor, digest pipeline, JSON-schema gateway, eight LLM adapters, audit log, cache, cloud-functions fallback, beautiful `IntelligenceBriefView`, and 26-kind widget registry all exist and work. What does **not** ship:

- **Verdict-first opening** — users land on placeholder text ("Tap the composer to investigate") instead of a real brief.
- **Auto-analysis cadence** — nothing fires until the user manually prompts; no daily brief, no weekly recap, no annual recap.
- **Real data behind half the widgets** — quota, anomaly, forecast, funnel, useCaseCluster, focus matrices are stubs because `keyTools` / `keyCommands` / `inferredTaskTitle` / `operatingActions` / `quotaBuckets` are never populated on the snapshot.
- **Drill-down that drills** — citations re-run the engine instead of opening the underlying session.
- **Off-tab surfaces** — no lock-screen widget, no Live Activity, no Watch/Wear complication, no menubar quick-brief, no notifications.
- **Shareable artifacts** — no PNG/PDF/MP4 export, no "Year in Coding" recap.
- **Charting on Android** — Canvas stubs, no real chart library.
- **Tool-use** — `InsightToolBroker` exists; adapters declare `supportsToolUse: true`; no adapter actually calls a tool.
- **Cross-platform parity** — iOS and Android each reimplemented the type system; an iOS canvas does not deserialize on Android.

This plan does three things and only three things:

1. **Rewires the in-tab surface** around a verdict-first editorial brief that auto-refreshes, drills into traces, and is always grounded in cited session IDs.
2. **Adds the cadence stack** — daily morning brief, weekly recap, monthly review, **annual Year-in-Coding** — plus all the off-tab surfaces (widgets, complications, notifications, share cards, PDFs, MP4).
3. **Closes the data and parity gaps** — populate the snapshot fields the executor needs, ship the stubbed widget bodies (mermaid, streamGraph, treemap, anomaly, forecast, funnel, quota, focuses), wire tool-use, ship a real Android chart library, and unify on a single canvas schema KMP can speak.

Acceptance bar: **a user who has never touched the tab opens it and within one minute either accepts a recommendation, drills into a session, or shares a card.** When all three are true on first-touch in <60s for 5 representative users, the plan is done.

## 1. Vision — what the tab actually *is*

**Insights is the editorial surface for your AI-assisted coding life.** It is *not* a BI tool. It is *not* a Chart Studio. It is *not* a dashboard builder. Those framings produce dashboard graveyards.

The mental model is **Apple Health × Linear Insights × Spotify Wrapped × Vercel Observability × Granola**:

- **Apple Health** — three daily rings; longitudinal trends that *gate* on enough data; step-function fits; "you averaged X, now you average Y" framing.
- **Linear Insights** — one opinionated verdict above the fold; restrained typography; no settings buried in tabs.
- **Spotify Wrapped** — annual narrative; vertical card-stack; shareable identity-framed moments.
- **Vercel Observability** — every agent session is a horizontal flame trace; the timeline *is* the insight.
- **Granola** — gray AI-authored text, black user-authored text; AI never speaks without citing.

It must work for someone who logs in for **5 seconds while walking** (lock-screen widget shows today's pacing ring), for **30 seconds at their desk** (menubar popover gives the daily verdict), for **3 minutes after a session** (the trace + a one-sentence "what happened"), for **20 minutes on a Sunday** (the weekly recap PDF), and for **one moment a year** (Year-in-Coding recap, designed to be screenshotted).

It must feel **like a peer told you something**, never like a robot generated a report.

## 3. Design principles — the taste contract

Lock these into every PR review.

### 3.1 The Verdict Above All
The first sentence of the tab is **always one specific claim with one number and one citation array**. No greeting, no "Welcome back", no spinner above 200ms. If the engine has not produced a fresh verdict, the cached one shows immediately with a `stale` chip and an unobtrusive refresh.

### 3.2 Schema-Locked Narrative
No free-form LLM text is ever rendered. Every narrative is the contents of a typed schema slot. The schema *is* the design system. Voice is enforced by post-processing:
- Banned phrases (case-insensitive): *Based on the data, It seems that, leveraging, significant, substantial, notable, considerable, robust, harness the power, in conclusion.*
- Banned construction: any sentence without a number, or any quantitative claim without a `sessionIds: [string]` citation. Drop the sentence; never hedge.
- Hard caps: headline ≤80 chars; body ≤90 words; bullets ≤14 words each; max 4 bullets.

### 3.3 Local-First, Hosted-Optional
Default narrator is on-device (Pi or Ollama) if reachable. Hosted (BurnBar OpenRouter→MiniMax fallback) is the second choice. Each card shows provenance: a subtle chip — "Authored locally by `qwen3-coder:30b` · $0 · 1.2s".

### 3.4 Charts Are Receipts
Every chart sits *under* a sentence. A user should be able to skim the sentences and ignore the charts. The charts are there to *prove* the sentences.

### 3.5 Drill-Down Or It Didn't Happen
Every quantitative claim is clickable. Tap reveals the rows; the rows reveal the session; the session reveals the trace; the trace reveals the prompts / tool calls / cost ticks. Stripe Sigma is the model.

### 3.6 First 500ms = Headline + Ring + Trace
Open Insights → see exactly three things above the fold:
1. **Today verdict** (one sentence).
2. **The three rings** (Spend within budget · Cache hit rate above target · Sessions logged).
3. **Yesterday's session-trace strip** (a horizontal mini flame-graph).
Everything else is below the fold.

### 3.7 Anti-Slop Visual Contract
- Warm-neutral surfaces (`#F7F7F5` light · `#1A1A1B` dark), never pure white or pure black.
- One display face (Bricolage Grotesque or Söhne Breit), one text face (system), one mono face (SF Mono / JetBrains Mono).
- Monospace numerals on every KPI digit.
- One dominant accent per provider (Anthropic = ember, OpenAI = whimsy, local = silver, Hermes = mercury). No indigo gradients. No `rounded-2xl shadow-sm` indigo cards.
- Asymmetric editorial grid: KPIs across the top, a hero card (the verdict), a secondary band (rings + trace), then the canvas. Not a sea of equal-sized cards.

### 3.8 Cadence Cap
Hard cap of **one push per day** (the daily brief) plus opportunistic anomaly pushes only when `z-score > 2 against same-day-of-week baseline`. Notifications are reciprocal: one accept-action per push so the user gains agency from each notification.

### 3.9 Trust Earned, Not Assumed
For the first 14 days of data, the tab is **facts only** — KPIs, comparisons, rings. Recommendations, forecasts, and trends unlock at thresholds (14d for trends, 30d for forecasts, 60d for recommendations). This mirrors Apple Health's 180d trends threshold.

### 3.10 Reduce-Motion Means Reduce Motion
Every animation has an `accessibilityReduceMotion` branch. Springs become fades. Stagger becomes simultaneous. No autoplay on the annual recap.

## 4. Architecture evolution

Most of v1.0 stays. We extend, we don't rebuild.

### 4.1 The canonical canvas — shared across all platforms

**Problem today:** iOS uses Swift `OpenBurnBarCore.SharedModels.Insights`; Android reimplemented every type in Kotlin under `com.openburnbar.data.insights`. A canvas authored on one cannot load on the other.

**Fix:** Treat the v1.0 Swift types in `OpenBurnBarCore/SharedModels/Insights/*` as the **wire schema**. Generate Kotlin equivalents from JSON Schema. Add a `tools/insights-schema-gen` Swift package executable that emits:
- `tools/insights-schema/build/insights.schema.json` (canonical)
- `tools/insights-schema/build/InsightsModels.kt` (data-class-generated, hand-edit forbidden)
- `tools/insights-schema/build/insights.d.ts` (for Cloud Functions and the website)

Wire it into `make insights-schema` and CI. Android's `:data:insights` module *consumes* the generated file; the existing Kotlin types are deleted in favor of generated ones over Phase B (with a deprecation shim).

### 4.2 The verdict pipeline (new)

Today: `InsightsViewModel.refreshSelectedCanvas()` is the only path; it does everything (digest → adapter → renderer) and is gated on user prompt.

New: split into a **two-tier verdict pipeline**:

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Tier 1: VerdictCache (always-on, instant)                      │
│   - Cached InsightVerdict per (deviceID, dayBucket)            │
│   - Read on tab open — < 16ms.                                 │
│   - If <2h old → render immediately, no spinner.               │
│   - If stale → render stale + kick Tier 2 in background.       │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                │
                                ▼
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Tier 2: VerdictComposer (background-refresh)                   │
│   - Build InsightDigest.                                       │
│   - Run RuleBased engine → produce InsightVerdict.local        │
│     (always; no LLM cost; guaranteed shape).                   │
│   - If a model is reachable, kick LLM authoring under          │
│     InsightVoiceSchemaV2 — narrative slots only.               │
│   - On completion, swap into VerdictCache and animate in.      │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                │
                                ▼
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Tier 3: ToolBrokerExploration (on demand)                      │
│   - User asks a follow-up.                                     │
│   - LLM adapter loops over InsightToolBroker.dispatch.         │
│   - Bounded by InsightInvestigationBudget (tokens, tools).     │
│   - Streams partials into the brief.                           │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why this matters:** The user always sees content within 16ms. The rule-based engine *guarantees a real verdict in the absence of any LLM*. The LLM is upgrade, never blocker.

### 4.3 InsightVerdict — the new top-level value

```swift
public struct InsightVerdict: Codable, Hashable, Sendable, Identifiable {
    public let id: UUID
    public var generatedAt: Date
    public var window: VerdictWindow                 // .today | .yesterday | .thisWeek | .lastWeek | .thisMonth | .quarter | .year
    public var headline: String                      // ≤80 chars, declarative
    public var subhead: String?                      // ≤120 chars
    public var rings: [VerdictRing]                  // exactly 3
    public var keyNumbers: [VerdictNumber]           // 3–4 KPIs, each with delta + sparkline
    public var sessionTrace: VerdictTraceStrip?      // yesterday's most consequential session
    public var bullets: [VerdictBullet]              // 1–4 specific claims, each with citations
    public var anomaly: VerdictAnomaly?              // surface only if z>2
    public var recommendation: VerdictRecommendation? // surface only with accept-action
    public var moodSwatch: ProviderTint              // colors the hero
    public var provenance: InsightModelTag           // who authored
    public var confidence: InsightConfidence
    public var followUps: [String]                   // 3 suggested next questions
}
```

This is the *only* thing the verdict pipeline produces. The canvas grid below it is still v1.0 widgets — but the verdict is *always* present, *always* fresh-or-stale, and *always* the dominant frame.

### 4.6 The session-trace primitive (new)

This is the single highest-leverage new visual. Vercel-style.

```swift
public struct VerdictTraceStrip: Codable, Hashable, Sendable {
    public let sessionID: String
    public var lanes: [TraceLane]          // model, tool, cache, prompt, response
    public var ticks: [TraceTick]          // cost ticks every N seconds
    public var startedAt: Date
    public var endedAt: Date
    public var summary: String             // ≤80 chars; "Refactored 4 files; 3 cache hits; 1 retry."
    public var deltaUsd: Decimal
    public var didTimeout: Bool
}
```

Rendered as a horizontal flame-graph strip at the top of the verdict card. Tap → full session view with all events. Lives in `OpenBurnBarCore/Views/Insights/InsightSessionTraceView.swift` and is shared.

### 4.7 Tool-use, finally

`InsightToolBroker` exists and is read-only. Adapters declare `supportsToolUse: true` but never call it. Wire it:

- `AnthropicInsightAdapter.analyze()` and `.investigate()` gain a tool-loop:
  1. Pass `InsightToolDefinitions.all` as `tools` in the request.
  2. On `.toolUse` blocks, call `InsightToolBroker.dispatch`; feed back as `.toolResult`.
  3. Cap at 6 tool calls per investigation (`InsightInvestigationBudget.maxToolCalls`).
- `OpenAIInsightAdapter` and `OpenRouterInsightAdapter` use function-calling API.
- Hermes/BurnBar-hosted Cloud Function gains a `proxy` that forwards tool calls to a server-side broker that hits Firestore directly with the user's UID; same 6-call cap.

Tool surface stays read-only by construction.

### 4.8 KMP candidate: `:core:insights-models`

Phase E creates a Kotlin Multiplatform module that contains *only* the generated insight models (from §4.1) plus the executor's pure functions. iOS imports it via XCFramework; Android consumes natively; daemon may consume via KMP/JVM. This kills the duplicate-types problem permanently. Scope is intentionally minimal — no SwiftUI, no Compose, no I/O.

## 5. The four surfaces

We design the tab as one surface within a four-surface system. Every insight knows which surface it can appear on; the renderer routes accordingly.

### 5.1 In-tab — the editorial brief

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Insights                          [Today ▾]  [Model ▾]  [⋯]    │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                  │
│  "You spent $4.12 yesterday — 28% under your 4-week average,    │  ← THE VERDICT
│   driven by a cache hit rate of 91% on the Atlas refactor."     │     (always present,
│                                                                  │      always cited)
│  Authored locally by qwen3-coder:30b · 1.4s · 0 tokens out       │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  ◯ Spend     ◯ Cache     ◯ Sessions    ▰▱▱▱▱▱▱▱▰▱▱▱▰  Yesterday │  ← RINGS + TRACE
│  $4.12/$12   91%/85%     3/2            14:32 → 14:51            │     (peripheral
│                                          $1.47 · 3 cache hits     │      vision band)
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  • 53% of your Sonnet calls were < 500 input tokens.            │  ← BULLETS
│    Haiku would have saved $14 this week.    [Switch default]    │     (recommendation
│  • Your local Pi handled 38% of insights — saving $1.10.        │      surfaces here
│  • Cache hit rate on agentlens-mobile dropped from 89→62%.      │      iff present)
│    Likely cause: new branch without `--cache`. [Show diff]      │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                  │
│  [Canvas grid — v1.0 widgets, lazy-loaded, scroll]              │  ← THE CANVAS
│                                                                  │     (now secondary)
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Cross-platform mapping:**

- **macOS:** three-pane workspace stays (sidebar + verdict-and-canvas + inspector). Verdict gets pinned above canvas grid. Sidebar gains a "Briefs" section (Today · Yesterday · This Week · This Month · This Year) above the saved canvases.
- **iPad:** NavigationSplitView; sidebar shows brief navigation + saved canvases; detail shows verdict + grid; inspector is a sheet. iPad picks up **Pencil annotation** on any chart and **drag chart to other app**.
- **iPhone:** scroll structure: verdict (1 screen) → rings strip → bullets → canvas grid (lazy). Composer is a floating pill at the bottom. Pull-down to refresh.
- **Android:** same scroll structure; uses Material 3 Expressive components; canvas grid is a Compose `LazyVerticalGrid` with Vico-rendered cards.

**State machine for the verdict surface:**
1. `firstLaunch` → show **demo verdict** generated from anonymized sample data, with a single "Run on your data" CTA.
2. `dataPresent, neverAnalyzed` → show **rule-based local verdict immediately**; kick off LLM authoring; render stale chip while in-flight.
3. `dataPresent, recentVerdict` → render cached; check freshness; refresh if >2h or `lastActivityAt > verdict.generatedAt + 30m`.
4. `dataPresent, stale > 24h` → render cached with a `stale` banner and a `refresh` chip; auto-refresh on appear.
5. `error` → render last good verdict + an unintrusive footer with retry; never blank the screen.

### 5.3 Cadence — daily, weekly, monthly, annual

| Cadence | Artifact | Authored at | Delivered as | Renderer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Daily** | Morning brief (verdict + 3 bullets + ring deltas) | 07:00 local (configurable) | Push notification + in-tab pinned + email (opt-in) | `MorningBriefRenderer` |
| **Weekly** | Sunday recap — Numbers / Wins / Surprises / Risks / Try next week | Sunday 18:00 local | In-tab pinned for a week + PDF export + email (opt-in) | `WeeklyRecapRenderer` |

| **Real-time** | Anomaly callout, milestone, risk | Event-driven | Push + in-tab top-of-feed | `RealtimeInsightRenderer` |

**Locked schema for the weekly recap** (this is the most important enforcement — predictability builds trust):

```
Week of {dateRange}
─────────────────────────
NUMBERS
  • Spend: ${this} ({delta} vs last week)
  • Sessions: {n} ({delta})
  • Cache hit: {pct}% ({delta})

WINS
  • {one specific outcome with sessionIds}

SURPRISES
  • {one anomaly or pattern the user wouldn't have noticed}

RISKS
  • {quota / spend pace / cache decay / secret leak}

TRY NEXT WEEK
  • {one accept-able recommendation}
```

Same shape every week. Comparable across weeks. Easier to skim.

### 5.4 Share — every insight is an exportable artifact

Every verdict, every bullet, every chart has a **"Share as card"** action that produces:

- A **1080×1350 PNG** (Instagram-friendly portrait) with the headline, the chart, and a small "OpenBurnBar · burnbar.ai" footer.
- A **1080×1080 PNG** square variant.
- An **A4 PDF** for any weekly/monthly recap.
- A **9:16 MP4** for the Year-in-Coding (per-card 4s with `AVFoundation` on Apple, MediaCodec on Android).

PNGs carry hidden EXIF metadata with the source `sessionIDs` so the share recipient could (in theory) deep-link back — but more pragmatically, so the user can re-find the source later.

Implementation: a single `InsightShareCardRenderer` in `OpenBurnBarCore/Views/Insights/Share/` that takes any `InsightVerdict` or `InsightWidget` and emits the four artifacts.

## 6. AI narrative contract — `InsightVoiceSchemaV2`

The contract is the spine of trust.

### 6.1 The schema

```jsonc
{

  "$id": "https://burnbar.ai/insights/voice-v2.schema.json",
  "type": "object",
  "required": ["headline", "subhead", "bullets", "provenance", "confidence"],
  "properties": {
    "headline": { "type": "string", "maxLength": 80 },

    "bullets": {

      "minItems": 1, "maxItems": 4,

        "type": "object",
        "required": ["claim", "type", "citations"],

            "type": "object",

              "payload": { "type": "object" }
            },
            "required": ["label","intent"]
          }
        }
      }
    },
    "provenance": {
      "type": "object",
      "required": ["modelID","providerKey","egressTier"],
      "properties": {
        "modelID":     { "type": "string" },
        "providerKey": { "type": "string" },
        "egressTier":  { "enum": ["localOnly","userKey","userRelay","hosted"] }
      }
    },
    "confidence": { "enum": ["low","med","high"] }
  }
}
```

### 6.2 Prompt contract

The prompt is built deterministically by `InsightPromptEngine.buildVerdictPrompt(digest:window:taxonomy:)`. Sections:

1. **System** — voice contract (banned phrases list, sentence rules, citation requirement). Tone exemplars.
2. **Schema** — the JSON Schema above, embedded; "you will be rejected if you violate it."
3. **Digest** — the privacy-bounded digest (<24KB) from `InsightDigestBuilder`.
4. **Window** — what timeframe.
5. **Prior** — last verdict for this window (so the model can compare and avoid repeating).
6. **Taxonomy** — the controlled vocabulary of focuses, use-cases, providers, models (so output is comparable week-over-week).
7. **Goal** — produce one `InsightVerdict` instance.

### 6.3 Voice exemplars (in the prompt)

```
GOOD:
  "You spent $4.12 yesterday — 28% under your 4-week average,
   driven by 91% cache hit on the Atlas refactor."

BAD:
  "It seems your spending has been notable this week, with significant
   improvements in cache utilization."
   (banned: It seems, notable, significant; no number; no citation)

GOOD:
  "53% of your Sonnet calls were under 500 input tokens — Haiku would
   have saved $14 this week."  [type: recommendation, acceptAction:
   switchRouterRule, citations: [s_a91…, s_b12…, …]]

BAD:
  "Consider leveraging smaller models for routine tasks."
   (banned: leveraging; no number; no citation; no accept action)
```

### 6.4 Post-processing

`InsightVoicePostProcessor` runs every LLM output through:
- Banned-phrase regex; drop offending sentences.
- Each `claim` must contain ≥1 numeric token; otherwise drop.
- `citations` must reference session IDs present in the digest; otherwise drop.
- `acceptAction.intent` must be in the registry; otherwise demote to no-action bullet.
- If <1 bullet survives, fall back to the rule-based engine's verdict and surface a low-confidence chip on the card.

### 6.5 Confidence calibration

`confidence` is enforced by the post-processor, not the model:
- `high` ⇔ digest had ≥30d data AND every bullet has citations AND no `acceptAction` modifies user defaults.
- `low` ⇔ digest had <14d data OR any bullet has 1 citation only OR window is `today`.
- Otherwise `med`.

Visual mapping: low = italic gray; med = regular; high = semibold. Subtle, never alarming.

## 8. Phased execution

**One branch, one body of work.** Phases are for self-management, not for a series of PRs.

### Phase A — Verdict-first surface (the heartbeat)
**Goal:** opening the tab on any platform shows a real verdict in <500ms.

- Add `InsightVerdict` + `VerdictRing` + `VerdictNumber` + `VerdictBullet` + `VerdictTraceStrip` + `VerdictAnomaly` + `VerdictRecommendation` types in `OpenBurnBarCore/SharedModels/Insights/Verdict/`.
- Add `VerdictCache` actor + `VerdictComposer` actor in `OpenBurnBarCore/Services/Insights/Verdict/`.
- Add `RuleBasedVerdictEngine` (always-on local baseline).
- Rewire `InsightsMacEnvironment.onAppear` and `InsightsStore.loadInitial` to load verdict from cache → render → background-refresh.
- Replace all hard-coded "Tap the composer…" placeholders.
- Build `VerdictHeroView` (shared) + `VerdictRingsStrip` (shared) + `VerdictBulletList` (shared).
- macOS: pin verdict above canvas grid.
- iPad/iPhone: verdict on top of scroll; canvas grid below.
- Android: same scroll structure in Compose.
- First-run demo verdict using `InsightVerdictDemoFixture`.
- Reduce-motion paths for every animation.

**Definition of done:** open the tab cold on macOS, iPhone, iPad, Android → verdict visible <500ms, no placeholder text, no spinner above 200ms.

### Phase B — Snapshot enrichment + real data behind every widget
**Goal:** every stubbed widget renders real data.

- Daemon: new `session_tool_rollup` writer (joins `operating_action_history` → per-session top tools, commands, files).
- macOS `DataStore`: expose `fetchSessionMetadata(window:) -> [InsightSessionRow]` with `inferredTaskTitle`, `keyTools`, `keyCommands`, `keyFiles`.
- iOS/Android `FirestoreRepository`: same surface from Firestore rollups (new functions endpoint to compute server-side if needed).
- `MacInsightDataSource.snapshot()` populates `quotaBuckets`, `operatingActions`, `summaryRuns`.
- `MobileInsightDataSource.snapshot()` same.
- `InsightExecutor.makeForecast()` → Holt-Winters; gate <30d.
- `InsightExecutor.makeAnomalyTable()` → robust z-score, day-of-week normalized.
- `InsightExecutor.makeFunnel()` → real funnel.
- `InsightExecutor.makeQuota()` → real gauge.
- `InsightExecutor.makeUseCaseCluster()` → server-clustered ID lookup (new functions endpoint `insightsUseCaseClusters` with daily caching).
- Renderers: `streamGraph`, `treemap`, `mermaid` (WebKit) all ship.
- Android: introduce **Vico** (Apache 2) charting; replace Canvas stubs for time series, ranking, heatmap, scatter, area, sankey-light.
- `tools/insights-schema-gen` Swift package emits canonical JSON Schema + Kotlin + TypeScript on `make insights-schema`; wire into CI.
- Android `:data:insights` consumes generated Kotlin models; old duplicates deleted in same change.

**Definition of done:** every widget kind renders real, non-empty data on a representative 90-day fixture; canvases authored on iOS deserialize on Android byte-for-byte.

### Phase C — Drill-down + tool-use
**Goal:** "every quantitative claim is clickable."

- `InsightSessionTraceView` (shared) — full flame-graph for a session.
- Citation chips on iOS/iPad/macOS/Android route to `openSession(sessionID)` → presents `InsightSessionTraceView` modally / in inspector.
- "Why?" tap on every KPI tile → calls `VerdictComposer.explainKPI(kpiID:)` → local Pi/Ollama → short tooltip narrative (cached).

- OpenAI/OpenRouter adapter: function-calling parity.
- Hermes / BurnBar-hosted Cloud Function: proxy mode for tool calls.
- macOS command palette (⌘K) routes natural-language insights queries.
- Drill-to-SQL on macOS (right-click any KPI → "Open as SQL").

**Definition of done:** every visible number can be tapped through to the rows; tool-use answers a question requiring drilldown in ≤8s for fast models.

### Phase D — Cadence stack
**Goal:** Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Annual all ship.

- `MorningBriefScheduler` (macOS daemon) + UNUserNotification (iOS) + FCM (Android). 07:00 local default; respects "Do Not Disturb".
- `WeeklyRecapRenderer` + locked schema + PDF + opt-in email via Firebase Functions.

- `YearInCodingRenderer` — vertical card-stack, motion via SwiftUI `phaseAnimator` / Compose `AnimatedContent`, music optional, MP4 + per-card PNG export.

- Milestone push channel (opt-out per category).
- Settings hub gets a new "Insights & cadence" section.

**Definition of done:** a user can opt in to email, gets a daily push in the morning, a Sunday recap, a 1st-of-month review, and can preview their Year-in-Coding at any time.

### Phase E — Off-tab surfaces
**Goal:** the verdict surfaces wherever the user is.

- iOS Lock-Screen widget + StandBy widget (small + rectangular).

**Definition of done:** any verdict bullet can be shared as a beautifully-designed PNG in ≤2 taps.

### Phase G — Polish & ship
- Pin / hide / promote affordances on every bullet and widget.
- Achievement system (3 only).
- Local-only mode toggle in settings; egress tier badges everywhere.
- Accessibility pass: every chart `accessibilityLabel` + `accessibilityValue`; TalkBack + VoiceOver tested.

- Documentation: `docs/INSIGHTS.md`, `docs/INSIGHTS_ARCHITECTURE.md`, `docs/INSIGHTS_VOICE.md`.
- `CHANGELOG.md`.

## 11. Risks, trade-offs, and open questions

### 11.1 Risks
- **LLM output drift.** Even with schema-locked generation and banned-phrase enforcement, models occasionally produce low-signal bullets. *Mitigation:* `InsightVoicePostProcessor` strips offending bullets; rule-based engine always provides a guaranteed-quality baseline; fall back if <1 LLM bullet survives.
- **Cost creep.** Daily auto-investigations multiply usage. *Mitigation:* default narrator is local Pi/Ollama; hosted only when reachable user-key exists; cache TTL set per-window (today: 2h, week: 1d, month: 7d, year: 30d).
- **Notification fatigue.** Daily + anomaly + milestone can stack. *Mitigation:* hard cap 1/day baseline; anomaly only z>2; milestones opt-out per category.
- **Annual recap timing.** Year-in-Coding only useful if data history exists; first launch shows "Year-in-Coding unlocks when you've been logging for 60 days."
- **Tool-use latency.** 6-call cap, parallel tool dispatch within a turn, ≤8s total budget.
- **KMP refactor.** Phase E `:core:insights-models` is the riskiest item — generated types alone (Phase B) eliminates 80% of the duplication pain, so we can defer full KMP if scope tightens.

### 11.2 Trade-offs
- **Verdict-first vs. canvas-first.** The plan picks verdict-first. Power users who want the canvas can scroll. A "verdict-only" preference is *not* surfaced — the verdict is non-negotiable above the fold.
- **Annual recap as MP4 vs. interactive story.** MP4 is shippable, screenshot-able, share-able. Interactive story is a future Phase H.
- **Server-side use-case clustering vs. on-device.** Server-side wins on consistency and is the right choice; on-device fallback (small k-means) only when offline.
- **Vico on Android vs. AndroidX Charts (experimental).** Vico is mature, Apache-2, Compose-native, ships today.

### 11.3 Open questions for the user
1. **Email channel.** Sunday recap email is opt-in. Use Firebase Functions + SendGrid (existing infra), or skip email until a separate plan? *Default: opt-in email via existing infra.*
2. **Year-in-Coding cadence.** December drop on the 15th? Or "your year" each anniversary of first session? *Default: anniversary of first session — feels more personal.*
3. **Peer benchmark cohort.** Worth shipping in this plan, or defer to a follow-on? Needs anonymized cohort backend. *Default: defer; ship a placeholder "coming soon" tile so the UX has space for it.*
4. **Watch / Wear targets.** Are these in scope now or follow-on? *Default: in scope — Phase E.*
5. **macOS menubar replacement.** The existing menubar already does quota / spend; we add insights to it. Confirm not a separate menubar app. *Default: extend existing.*

If no objection within 24h of plan approval, the defaults stand.

## 12. Why this plan is the right move

| Decision | Alternative | Why we chose |
|---|---|---|
| Verdict-first, canvas-second | Keep canvas-first; let verdict be a widget | Canvas-first is what shipped and failed. Verdict-first is the editorial frame proven by Linear/Health/Wrapped. |
| Schema-locked narrative with banned-phrase regex | Free-form LLM output | Eliminates AI-slop; makes voice consistent; gives the post-processor teeth. |
| Local-first narrator | Hosted default | Trust, cost, latency. Hosted is the upgrade. |
| Rule-based engine as guaranteed baseline | LLM-only authoring | LLM unavailability cannot break the surface. |
| Cadence stack with annual recap | In-tab only | Wrapped is the killer feature. Daily/weekly/monthly compound trust and identity. |
| Vico on Android | Custom Canvas charts forever | Real charts ship in a week; building our own swallows the entire Android budget. |
| Generated insight models (Swift→Kotlin→TS) | Hand-maintained per platform | Eliminates the source of cross-platform divergence pain. |
| Tool-use wired into adapters | Static digests forever | Lets the model answer follow-ups without bloating the digest; unlocks Tier-3 exploration. |
| Off-tab surfaces in this plan | "Phase 2" | Insights belongs where the user is; lock-screen widget is the highest-leverage off-tab moment. |
| Achievement system with three trophies only | Full gamification | Avoid the gamification trap; rare = meaningful. |
| Voice contract enforced by post-processor | Trust the model to be good | Models drift; regex doesn't. |
| One PR, internal phases | A series of PRs | The product only works when all phases are present; partial ships are the v1.0 failure repeating. |
